Graphic Design

Graphic Design is a natural extension of my passion for creativity and visual art. As a student at Davis Technical College and Weber State University, I enjoy exploring how strong visuals, meaningful layouts, and various forms of media can communicate the ideas folks try to get across to their audience.

My experience ranges from creating graphics for organizations, clubs, and websites down to stickers and waterslide decals for hobby projects. I've always been drawn to capturing interesting art through various forms. I have a passion for photography and fine-detailed model making as well. I'm constantly looking for projects to improve my skills and give me new challenges.

I have a strong interest in typography and how it can be used to enhance the message of a design. I enjoy experimenting with different fonts and layouts to create visually appealing and effective designs. I also have experience with branding and how to create a cohesive visual identity for a company or organization.

Other Tech Skills

I have experience with various technologies and tools, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and content management systems. I'm also familiar with version control systems like Git and have a basic understanding of web development including various levels of web accessibility.

I enjoy learning new technologies and finding ways to apply them to my projects. I'm always looking for opportunities to expand my skill set and take on new challenges in the tech world.

Case Study

This particular project stemmed from a font learning course, ITC Avant Garde Gothic Pro.

I wanted to create a design that would allow me to experiment with the font and its various weights and styles. I decided to create an NHL team branding for the at the time up and coming Utah Mammoth, prior to the name of course. Black Diamonds was one of the names in the voting and was my favorite of the bunch.

I also am very vocal about prefering the city's name over the state when it comes to events or teams, (ie, the 2034 Utah Olympics should be the 2034 Salt Lake City Olympics) so I wanted to incorporate that into the design as well.
